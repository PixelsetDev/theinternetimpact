Mental Health

The internet has changed Mental Health forever.

The positives.

  • Easily accessible support services for everyone.
  • Find new friends and communities, network with other people who you can talk to about your mental health.
  • Allows people to raise awareness of mental health issues.

The negatives.

  • Social media can damage your self image.
  • Cause FOMO, Isolation, Depression or Anxiety.
  • Allows more routes for Cyberbullying.

How you can improve your mental health online.

  • Take a break

    Switch off, make sure to take a break from social media - listen to some music, take a walk, or do something else offline.

  • Filter

    Some search services and websites now allow you to filter search results, removing topics that you may find distressing. An example is Google's Safe Search.

  • Restrict

    Block or restrict accounts and websites that damage your mental health, so you'll never see them in your feed.

  • Seek help

    If you're struggling with your mental health, talk to someone. See our Mental Health Support page.

  • Get support for your Mental Health.
